Almost everything in my portfolio is my own product: a subscription service with ~200 paying users, an AI CRM, a repricer covering 5,500 SKUs. All of it is running in production right now.

How I work

I take on the whole job — from core logic and payments to launching on the server. I build it myself and handle all the messaging personally: in plain language, no tech jargon.

I name a deadline and hold it. The date and price are fixed in the proposal before work starts, and they don't drift along the way.

I think in business, not just code. A product should make money or save it, not merely "work." If a feature doesn't pay off, I'll say so plainly — that's what happened with the repricer and the CRM, where good-looking features got cut because the numbers didn't add up.

I design for real people. I look at the product through the user's eyes, not the developer's: where they'll get stuck, what they'll abandon halfway, what they won't grasp without a hint.

I see it through. Most of my attention goes where projects usually die: taking payments, granting access, and getting to production.
