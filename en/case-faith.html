01 · problem

The topic has a high barrier to entry: the sources are either texts from two centuries ago that you have to decode first, or reference sites built like a library rather than a course. Someone ready to take a step googles their first question, gets three screens with twenty unfamiliar words, and closes the tab. A format that leads you from zero step by step doesn't exist in mobile form.

02 · solution

Flutter on iOS, fully offline-first: no backend, no servers, no auth — progress lives on the device. The content is a tree of courses where a lesson is theory slides plus a quiz. Any term in the text becomes a glossary card and unlocks as you read. Progress runs on XP, day streaks and “freezes” that don't reset the streak over a single missed day. Separately, a library of books: the manifest is pulled over the network, books download for offline use with sha256 integrity checks and stay on the device. The daily reminder is scheduled locally.

03 · what was hard

Formally done. In fact the key edit never got written at all: the editing tool aborted, and the other three referenced a value that was saved nowhere. The analyzer stayed silent — the field was formally used in the UI, and the compiler doesn't care that it's never written. The feature was dead with a fully green check. Caught only by reading the function body line by line.

Hence the rule: green checks prove the code compiles, not that it does what was intended. Now no change report is accepted without a verbatim dump of the changed fragment.

The right option was systematically the longest — the author padded it with an explanation and left the wrong ones terse, so a sharp reader could guess by shape without knowing the topic. The second, found on the device: the correct answer stood first in every question, because options rendered strictly by index.

Fixed in two layers. In code — shuffling the options, but not in the render method: it's called on every state update, and the buttons would jump under your finger. I had to find every transition between questions and shuffle there, and at the same time move five correctness checks from list position to the answer object itself. In content — a rule: options of equal length and grammatical form, the correct position spread evenly, wrong options are real beginner misconceptions, not straw men. Verified by a script at content build time.

Why both layers: shuffling makes the order in the source harmless for the app, but drafts are read by real people — author, reviewer, editor. A document where the correct answer is always first breeds blindness in everyone who proofreads it.

04 · result
  • 17 course branches, a glossary of 178 terms and 52 figures with inflected forms.
  • Flagship branch: 13 lessons, 52 slides, 39 quiz questions; the correct answers spread across positions — exactly a third each.
  • Every lesson passed an external expert review before publishing: 13 of 13, two mandatory fixes across the whole branch.
  • A library of six books with offline download and file-integrity checks.
  • Status: active development, no release yet.
